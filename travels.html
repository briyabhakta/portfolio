Travel Gallery

During the Summer of 2020, I studied abroad in Madrid for 6 weeks. Afterwords, my friend and I did a lot of travelling through Europe.

Madrid, Spain

I studied in Madrid for six weeks in May and June. I took two classes: one on old Spanish Literature, and one on the history of Madrid the city. I took this picture of Gran Vía, Madrid's most popular downtown street, from a rooftop bar.

Madrid, Spain

This is Puerta Del Sol, Madrid's most popular downtown square. Around this area are many stores and bakeries. I especialyl liked the the local stores, as I was able to try so many new things.

London, UK

London was amazing, and my favorite city I traveled to (outside Madrid, of course!) Here, I am outside of Buckingham Palace, where Queen Elizabeth lives. Sadly, she wasn't there when I visited, but I still had a great time; it was beautiful.

Rome, Italy

Although I didn't go inside the Coliseum, it was still amazing from the outside. Not only did I see beautiful things in Rome, but I also ate amazing things. I had the tiramisu in Rome. It was so interesting to see how all the old architecture built so long ago still remained intact with minimal reconstruction.

Edinburgh, Scotland

Scotland was amazing. The architecture was so different compared to all the places I went to. This picture is a street in Edinburgh looking onto Edinburgh Castle up on top of the hill. Although the weather was a bit rainy, I believe that it fit well with the nature of the city, and I would really love to go back there again.

Paris, France

Everything about Paris was just like I imagined. It was super hot, and we only had one day there, but I still had the best time. The Eiffel Tower was so much larger when I saw it in real life, and it looked truly amazing. In addition to the tower, I saw the pyramids at the Louvre, the Notre Dame, the Arc de Triomfe, and the Seine River.

Sevilla, Spain

Sevilla was the most beautiful city I have ever seen. The architecture was breathtaking. It was a combination of Christian and Moorish styles and it was extraordinary. This is a picture of the Plaza de España; it was incredibly large, and I could've spent hours there admiring its beauty.

Barcelona, Spain

Although we only had one day here, Barcelona was amazing and we saw so much. We mainly saw structures designed by Antoni Gaudí, and I was truly amazed by his architecture. This is the Sagrada Familia, a famous Basílica designed by Gaudí. Although unfinished, it is the most amazing Basílica I've ever seen. I loved the elements of nature and the modernity of the building; it was so unique, and nothing like I've ever seen before.

Lauterbrunnen, Switzerland

Switzerland was so beautiful. The nature had me speechless, and it was a nice change of pace from the bustling tourist cities we had traveled to in Europe. The town we went to was fairly secluded, and it was just a small village. It was nice and quiet, and as seen in this photo, there were so many beautiful waterfalls and a nice, flowing stream.
